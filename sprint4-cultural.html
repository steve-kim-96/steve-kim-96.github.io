What did I take away from the research?

I've gained a lot of insights about neuroplasticity and the "Growth Mindset", especially since we've already gone through
the same material. The one thing that stands out and is a relief to me is the TED Talk given by Dr. Carol Dweck. It was
about the fact that as long as you believe and know that you can improve and that nothing is impossible is the first
step taken towards growth. It really gave me a good mindset going forward because there are some challenges that I
couldn't solve with full confidence in the answer. What kept me going was that through reasoning, and trying to come up
with a solution, my mind is developing, creating links with what I've learnt through the online courses and videos and
working out a way to solve this problem.

In the end, I had a realization of how powerful our minds are, and that attitude is something that can really affect your
ability to learn. For example, if two people are stuck on the same problem, the person with a growth mindset and positive
attitude is likely to tackle the problem with good logical reasoning, steps and ways to get around the problem.
The other person, with a negative view (due to circumstance, life, etc.) is likely to look at the problem, give some options
that immediately come to mind, and start complaining or give up.

I've truly learnt what it means to learn through our struggles and that it's not necessarily the smartest person in the room
that sees the problem through, but whoever has the right mindset, developed skills as a result, and the determination to
see it through.

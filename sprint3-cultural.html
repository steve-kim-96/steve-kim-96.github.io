What's the difference between EQ and IQ?

Let's first discusss what they are. IQ, as we all know, stands for Intelligence Quotient.
IQ is a score derived from a set of standardized tests, which is designed to assess
human intelligence. EQ stands for Emotional Quotient. EQ is the ability of individuals
to understand their own emotions, as well as the emotions of those around them.

EQ is important to me because it doesn't just take smarts to get through a job, or even
life. If I understand my emotions, why I'm feeling happy, relieved, sad or overwhelmed
Then I can figure out a game plan to tackle whatever is making me feel bad, or celebrate
why I'm feeling good. It's also important because if someone around me is feeling any
of those same emotions I can be happy for them or try help them get through whatever
it is that's making them feel bad.

This is all relevant to me, because being smart won't help me figure out the emotions of people.
If I ever come across an uncomfortable situation at EDA, for example, then I need to make sure I
have a method of working through those emotions.
